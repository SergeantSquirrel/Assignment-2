Compare and Contrast


The subsequent section will cover more analytics and data on our ideal jobs however it is beneficial to compare with a grounded perspective. By titles alone there is a wide array of jobs covered amongst the team. Interestly, not all are within the IT industry itself; highlighting Dat’s management accounting dream. It's not uncommon to see crossovers with other industries in the IT sector. As Jon Lang, CEO of DDLS points out ‘the simple fact is, there are nowhere near enough skilled professionals coming out of universities to fulfill all the IT roles needed in Australian organisations.’ As a result, it’s highly favoured and common to see other industries pursuing basic IT knowledge and skill to be able to relieve the demand on the IT sector.

It's important to highlight the importance of our personality results and the personal information given by the team members. No one person is the same as another; our interests, life circumstances, upbringing and the people around us are all contributing factors to who we are. It’s all relevant because to compare our ideal jobs, would be to compare us as individuals.

Focusing towards the personality results. It’s important to know and understand what they mean. In a group environment (this assessment included), understanding these results will help in developing your role within a team. Some are quiet, some are loud, some learn best through visuals, some like to take charge and others like to be given an independent task. It's not only helpful in understanding your role and how you work within a group environment, it’s helpful to those who work best in leadership roles. It can aid in identifying the “quiet members” or delegating work to the team to adhere to their strengths and in turn be more efficient.
